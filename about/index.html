Yaqiang Cao CV

Brief Bio

Yaqiang got his Ph.D. in Computational Biology from CAS-MPG Partner Institute for Computational Biology, Shanghai Institutes for Biological Sciences, under the supervision of Dr. Jing-Dong Jackie Han. He is now a bioinformatics Postdoc in NHLBI/NIH, under the mentoring of Dr. Keji Zhao.

Yaqiang has more than ten years of experience applying and analyzing deep sequencing technologies (RNA-Seq, ChIP-Seq, DNase-Seq, ATAC-seq, Hi-C, etc.) to understand transcription regulation during multiple biological processes of molecular working models. He focused on the epigenetic roles of human transposable elements during his Ph.D. student studies. Yaqiang is now working on understanding the chromatin interaction landscapes of human and mouse immune cells, especially on T cells. Collaborating closely with wet-lab mates, he analyzes and integrates sequencing data to intercept biology insights, and develops the analysis methods for new chromatin 1D and 3D profiling protocols.

Yaqiang’s initial research interests include data mining and machine learning from massive sequencing data, and bioinformatics tools development rooting in Dr. Han’s lab. His interests turnned to sequencing-based methodologies development and T-cells immunology during the Postdoc trainning with Dr. Zhao.

Yaqiang has published the first/co-first author’s research articles on Nucleic Acids Research (2022 and 2020), Blood (2020), Bioinformatics (2020) and Genome Research (2019); many other solid interesting research papers as contributing collaborator such as Nature Metabolism (2020), Science Advances (2020), Cell Reports (2019) and Cell Research (2015).

Yaqiang is also an active reviewer for Frontiers in Immunology, Frontiers in Pharmacology and BMC Genomics, happy to serve the community.

Outside the working time, Yaqiang is interested in the research topics he is not involved yet (not limited to biology). Diversity of knowledge is fantanstic.
